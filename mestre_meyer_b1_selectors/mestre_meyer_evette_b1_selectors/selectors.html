Julienne Elegance



A colorful blend of julienne vegetables makes an elegant, appetizing accompaniment to any entree. First, you'll need a very sharp chef's knife and a dry, stable cutting board. Next, make sure your selection of vegetables is washed well and thoroughly dried to prevent slipping while cutting. Here are instructions for different types of vegetables:

Carrots
Peel carrots with a vegetable peeler. Cut in half cross wise, then cut in half lengthwise. With the cut side facing down, carefully cut lengthwise slivers into the carrot. For a finer julienne, simply lay the slivers on their sides and repeat the process one more time.

Zucchini or Squash
Cut the ends off the vegetable. Cut in half cross wise. Then stand it up on cut side. Starting from the top down, carefully trim the colorful skin and a little less then a quarter of an inch of the pulp. Discard the inner pulp and seeds. Then lay the skins cut side down and slice into thin lengthwise slivers.

Peppers
Stand a pepper up on your cutting board. Slice down from the top to bottom. Avoid cutting too deep into the curvy centers. Lay each pepper slice cut side up and simply cut into thin lengthwise slivers.

Onions
Cut the ends off the onion. Stand it up and cut down the middle. Peel back the outer skin and lay cut side down on board. Firmly squeezing the sides so the onion layers don't come apart, carefully cut into thin lengthwise slivers.

Celery
Cut the ends off the celery. Lay curved side up on your board on an angle. Cut crosswise into thin slivers.

Thai Basil Chicken



Chef Tanni adds a touch of dry sherry and little spice to warm up this traditional dish.

Ingredients

  • 3 T coconut oil or vegetable oil
  • 2 T fresh ginger, peeled and minced
  • 6-8 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1 small pinch of red pepper flakes
  • 1 whole carrot, julienned
  • 2 stalks celery, julienned
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 1/2 yellow bell pepper, julienned
  • 1 lb raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/4 c organic fermented soy sauce
  • 2 T dry sherry
  • 1 lb uncooked chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 c fresh basil leaves, julienned

Instructions

In a large stirfry pan or chef's skillet, sautee ginger, garlic and red pepper flakes for one minute. Add in vegetables.

Once vegetables begin to soften, add soy, sherry and chicken. Mix well, reduce heat to low and cover pan. Let simmer for 5 minutes. If sauce is a little dry add a tablespoon of water and a tablespoon of soy sauce at a time until it suits your taste. Stir in fresh basil just before serving.

Serves 4

Accompaniments:
Brown rice
White rice
Thin wheat noodles

Pairings:
Chardonnay
Sauvignon Blanc
Lychee Martini
